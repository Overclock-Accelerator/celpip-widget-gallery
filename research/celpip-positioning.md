## 6. Test-format facts they cite

### CELPIP - General (the main 4-skill test)
- Total time: **"under 3 hours"** / **"under 2 hours and 50 minutes"** / **"approximately 3 hours"** (varies slightly by page)
- Done in **one sitting, no separate speaking session**
- **Listening: 46–55 minutes, 38 multiple-choice questions across 6 parts**
  - Part 1: Listening to Problem Solving (8 Q)
  - Part 2: Listening to a Daily Life Conversation (5 Q)
  - Part 3: Listening for Information (6 Q)
  - Part 4: Listening to a News Item (5 Q)
  - Part 5: Listening to a Discussion (8 Q)
  - Part 6: Listening to Viewpoints (6 Q)
- **Reading: 43–56 minutes, 38 multiple-choice questions across 4 parts**
  - Part 1: Reading Correspondence (11 Q)
  - Part 2: Reading to Apply a Diagram (8 Q)
  - Part 3: Reading for Information (9 Q)
  - Part 4: Reading for Viewpoints (10 Q)
- **Writing: 53 minutes, 2 tasks** — "Task 1: Writing an Email" and "Task 2: Responding to Survey Questions"
- **Speaking: 15 minutes, 8 tasks** — Giving Advice; Talking about a Personal Experience; Describing a Scene; Making Predictions; Comparing and Persuading; Dealing with a Difficult Situation; Expressing Opinions; Describing an Unusual Situation
- Note: "Test takers will be required to read answer choices in the Listening Test and questions in the Speaking Test."

### CELPIP - General LS (Citizenship)
- Total time: **"about 1h10m"** / **"approximately 1 hour"** / **"approximately 70 minutes"** (varies by page)
- 2 components: Listening + Speaking
- Listening 46–55 min (or 47–55 on the General-LS page); Speaking 15 min (or 15–20)

### Pricing (Canadian)
- CELPIP - General: **"$290 + Taxes"** (CELPIP-General page) — also seen as **"$295 + Taxes (where applicable)"** on Test Overview/Test Format pages (price changed Oct 1, 2024 per news post)
- CELPIP - General LS: **"$195 + Taxes"** (CELPIP-General-LS page) — also seen as **"$199 + Taxes"** on Test Overview/Test Format

- "This is the Canadian price, international pricing will vary."
- Transfer fee: **$50 CAD + tax** (if ≥7 calendar days before test); cancellation = 50% refund if ≥7 days before

### Test centres / availability claims (verbatim)

### Score / CLB / DHA / CEFR ranges
- CELPIP scale: **0 to 12 (with NA)** — calibrated against **Canadian Language Benchmarks (CLB) Levels 3 to 12**, against the **Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)**, and against **Australia DHA English proficiency levels**
- CELPIP scores valid for **2 years from test date**
- DHA equivalencies (verbatim from Score Concordance):
  - **Superior English** — at least CELPIP 10 Listening, 10 Reading, 12 Writing, 10 Speaking (or "Proficient English – At least CELPIP 10 for Writing")
  - **Proficient English** — at least CELPIP 9 Listening, 8 Reading, 8 Speaking, 10 Writing (varies by source)
  - **Competent English** — at least a CELPIP 7 for each component
  - **Vocational English** — at least a CELPIP 5 for each component
- Other scoring benchmarks named: FMRAC requires 9/9/9/9; CAPR requires 8/8/8/8; Ontario College of Teachers requires 7/7/7/8 (overall 8); Canadian PGWP (Degree programs) requires 7/7/7/7; TasTAFE Australia requires 5/5/5/5 overall 7

## 9. Voice & tone observations

- **Sentence length:** Short to medium. Most hero/value-prop sentences are one clause, 12–22 words. Pillar copy is fragment-style ("Self-paced test is completed during one sitting in 3 hours or less with results in 2–4 days").
- **Pronoun usage:**
  - **"Your" / "you"** dominates user-facing pages (homepage, country pages, AU visas). Examples: "Your gateway to global opportunity", "Take **your** CELPIP English exam", "**Your** CELPIP English language results are officially accepted by IRCC", "Achieve **your** immigration goals with confidence."
  - **"We"** appears in support/comfort context: "**We** are proudly offering computer-delivered tests…", "**We** are excited to support our test takers…", "**We** continue to invest substantial resources…" (About Us).
  - **Imperative** for CTAs: "Find a Test Date", "Practice Now", "Sign Up Now", "Register Now", "Shop Now", "Learn More", "Explore Test Locations".
- **Formality level:** Professional but accessible. No casual slang; no exclamation marks except in promo banners ("Save 50% on CELPIP Study Materials!", "Get ready for CELPIP with hours of free prep!"). Formal in legal/policy/score-user pages; warmer in country and AU pages.
- **Distinctive phrases that recur (use these for brand consistency):**
  - "Accelerate your future"
  - "Your gateway to global opportunity"
  - "Achieve your immigration goals with confidence"
  - "What are you waiting for?"
  - "Why test takers trust CELPIP" (testimonial section heading)
  - "high-stakes social, educational, or workplace contexts" (score descriptors — repeated dozens of times across score chart pages)
  - "real social, educational, or workplace contexts" (score-user pages — paired phrase)
  - "the leading general English language proficiency test"
  - "officially designated" / "officially accepted" / "officially recognized" (used for IRCC/DHA — slight variation but always with "officially")
  - "computer-delivered tests done in one sitting with quick online results"
  - "single sitting" / "one sitting" — high-frequency convenience claim
  - "immigration journey" — used as the emotional throughline
- **Numerical claims pattern (always written as a range, low-friction):**
  - "2–4 days" (results)
  - "3-4 business days" (results, on India and overview pages)
  - "less than 3 hours" / "under 3 hours" / "approximately 3 hours"
  - "200+ centres" / "more than 200" / "40+ countries" / "over 40"
  - "100+ hours of free prep"
  - "10+ hours of free webinars"
  - "50+ instructional videos"
- **Testimonial blocks** — always identical two quotes (Chrisna D. and Rafaela B.) used as global footer testimonials site-wide:
  - **Chrisna D.:** "I had taken other English language proficiency before, and CELPIP was more relatable to me. All of the questions were situations I was familiar with from daily life, and were like conversations I had experienced personally."

## 10. Things they DON'T say (anti-patterns)

- **No immigration advice.** Site never tells users which visa to apply for or interprets IRCC requirements. Always defers: "For specific language requirements, please visit the IRCC website at www.cic.gc.ca."
- **No comparison to competitors by name.** No mention of IELTS, PTE, TOEFL, Duolingo English Test. About Us refers vaguely to "our principal competitors" and "international testing agencies" but never names them.
- **No guaranteed-score / pass-rate claims.** No "X% of test takers pass" or "achieve your target score in 30 days" guarantees. Only "many test takers find that enrolling in a preparation program… helps them reach their target scores" (qualified).
- **No urgency/scarcity language.** No "Only X seats left", no "Spots fill fast" — just "Secure an available test date within 2 weeks in most major cities."
- **No discount-led messaging on hero.** Sale banners exist ("Save 50% on CELPIP Study Materials!") but never replace the primary IRCC/DHA-recognition framing.
- **No testimonial clutter.** Only the same two quotes (Chrisna D., Rafaela B.) appear site-wide. No long carousel of stories.
- **No price on hero.** Pricing only appears on product detail pages (CELPIP-General, CELPIP-General LS, country pages), never on homepage hero.
- **Quebec carve-out is honest:** "Currently, the CELPIP-General Test is **not considered a designated English language test by the Quebec Immigration Office**. Quebec establishes its own immigration requirements."
- **No "Canadian English" tagline.** Despite the name, the site does NOT use a "100% Canadian English" or "Canadian-English-focused" headline tagline. Identity comes from "developed at the University of British Columbia" and "real-world communication" framing — not linguistic chauvinism.
- **No celebrity endorsements** or influencer marketing mentioned anywhere.
- **No cashback/free-money headline.** The National Bank "$300 cashback" promo is a partner banner, not a CELPIP claim — clearly marked as a third-party offer.
- **Hedge language used sparingly but consistently** when needed: "where applicable" (taxes), "in most major cities", "may vary", "International pricing will vary." Not used as marketing softeners on benefits.

### Notes for the next agent

1. **Tone calibration:** When in doubt, default to second-person, present tense, short sentence + range-stated number ("results in 2–4 days", "200+ centres in 40+ countries"). This matches the homepage register exactly.
2. **Recognition language is rigid and repeated.** Always pair "IRCC" with "approved by" or "officially accepted by" or "officially designated"; always pair "DHA" with "officially recognized" or "DHA-accepted". Don't substitute looser verbs ("recognised", "accredited", "endorsed") — celpip.ca avoids those.
3. **The hero CTA is always "Find a Test Date".** Don't invent new primary CTA verbs across our 10 microsites unless the page has a specific lower-funnel purpose (e.g., Resource Hub → "Practice Now" / "Sign Up Now").
4. **The closing module ("What are you waiting for?") is reusable as-is** on at least 6 of our 10 microsites.
5. **Country pages are tonally lighter** than the corporate pages — they pair the standard recognition framing with a city-specific test-finder. We can lean into city/state names in our regional microsites without straying off-brand.
